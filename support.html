‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

How do I add a new medication?
  1. Open the Medications tab at the bottom of the screen
  2. Tap the "+" button in the top right corner
  3. Enter the medication name, type, and timing preferences
  4. Configure reminder settings and optional stock tracking
  5. Tap "Save" to add the medication to your list
How do reminders work?

Medrix uses iOS local notifications to remind you to take your medications. You can set:

  • Daily: Same time every day
  • Every Other Day: Alternating days
  • Weekly: Once per week
  • Custom Days: Specific days of the week

Note: Make sure you've granted notification permissions in iOS Settings ‚Üí Medrix ‚Üí Notifications.

How do I mark a dose as taken?

In the Today tab, you'll see all scheduled medications for the current day. Simply tap the "Take" button on a medication card. You can also:

  • Mark as "Missed" if you skip a dose
  • View past days using the week calendar at the top
  • See when you actually took each dose (timestamp recorded)
Does my data sync across devices?

Yes! Medrix uses iCloud to automatically sync your medications, intake logs, and settings across all your Apple devices signed in with the same Apple ID. This happens seamlessly in the background.

Make sure iCloud is enabled in iOS Settings ‚Üí [Your Name] ‚Üí iCloud ‚Üí Medrix.

How do I manage medication stock?

When adding or editing a medication:

  1. Enable "Track Stock" in the medication form
  2. Enter your current stock count
  3. Set a low stock alert threshold

Each time you mark a dose as taken, the stock automatically decreases by 1. You'll receive an alert when stock reaches your threshold.

Can I track medications for family members?

Currently, Medrix is designed for individual use. Each person should use their own device and Apple ID to maintain privacy and accurate tracking. You can add notes to medications to indicate who they're for, but separate profiles are not yet supported.

What do the meal timing options mean?
  • Before Meal: Take 30-60 minutes before eating
  • With Meal: Take during or immediately after eating
  • After Meal: Take 30-60 minutes after eating
  • Empty Stomach: Take when you haven't eaten for 2+ hours
  • Anytime: Can be taken regardless of meals

Note: Always follow your healthcare provider's specific instructions.

How do I edit or delete a medication?

To Edit:

  1. Go to the Medications tab
  2. Tap on the medication you want to edit
  3. Tap the "Edit" button in the top right
  4. Make your changes and tap "Save"

To Delete:

  1. In the Medications list, swipe left on the medication
  2. Tap the "Delete" button
  3. Confirm deletion (this will also remove all associated intake logs)
What are Critical Reminders?

Critical reminders are a special type of notification for important medications that:

  • Play a louder, more persistent sound
  • Appear even when your device is in Do Not Disturb mode
  • Are harder to dismiss accidentally

Use this feature for time-sensitive or critical medications where missing a dose could have serious consequences.

Can I export my medication history?

Currently, Medrix doesn't have a built-in export feature. If you need your data for medical records, please contact us at talipdurmus1@gmail.com and we can assist you with extraction options.

üîß Troubleshooting

Notifications Not Working

Solution:
  1. Open iOS Settings ‚Üí Notifications ‚Üí Medrix
  2. Ensure "Allow Notifications" is ON
  3. Set notification style to "Banners" or "Alerts"
  4. Enable "Lock Screen", "Notification Center", and "Banners"
  5. Make sure "Do Not Disturb" isn't blocking notifications (unless using Critical Reminders)
  6. Verify the medication has "Reminder Enabled" turned on in the app

Data Not Syncing Across Devices

Solution:
  1. Ensure all devices are signed in with the same Apple ID
  2. Go to iOS Settings ‚Üí [Your Name] ‚Üí iCloud
  3. Verify iCloud Drive is enabled
  4. Scroll down and make sure Medrix is toggled ON
  5. Check you have sufficient iCloud storage available
  6. Try force-quitting and reopening the app on all devices

App Crashing or Freezing

Solution:
  1. Force close the app (swipe up from app switcher)
  2. Restart your device
  3. Make sure you're running the latest version of iOS
  4. Check for Medrix app updates in the App Store
  5. If the problem persists, contact support with details about when the crash occurs

Stock Count Not Decreasing

Solution:
  • Stock only decreases when you mark a dose as "Taken" (not "Missed")
  • Ensure "Track Stock" is enabled for that medication
  • Check that the stock count isn't already at zero
  • Try editing the medication and manually adjusting the stock if needed

Can't Find a Specific Day's History

Solution:
  • In the Today tab, use the week calendar at the top to navigate to different days
  • Swipe left/right on the calendar to see previous/future weeks
  • The Insights tab shows aggregate statistics over longer periods

üí° Tips & Best Practices

Setting Up for Success

  • Start Simple: Add one medication at a time and get comfortable with the interface
  • Use Notes: Add important information like dosage amounts or special instructions in the notes field
  • Enable Critical Reminders Wisely: Reserve for truly critical medications to avoid notification fatigue
  • Set Realistic Stock Alerts: Set your low stock alert with enough lead time to refill prescriptions
  • Review Weekly: Check your insights regularly to maintain good adherence

Privacy & Security

  • All your data is stored securely on your device and iCloud
  • Enable Face ID/Touch ID lock on your device for added security
  • We never share your health information with third parties
  • Read our Privacy Policy for full details
